# Realistic Photography Rules

Use these rules for every prompt. This skill's default is photorealism, not stylization.

## Core law

The image must look like a real camera captured a real moment.

Even when the subject is futuristic, sci-fi, supernatural, theatrical, or visually spectacular, it must follow real camera laws:
- believable lens perspective
- physically motivated light
- real shadows and contact points
- real reflections and occlusion
- real exposure limits
- natural material behavior
- human anatomy and performance grounded in reality

## AIMV / AI short-drama realism

Prompts should feel like production stills, film frames, live-event photos, behind-the-scenes captures, or documentary-style camera moments.

Prefer:
- real locations, built sets, practical stage installations, LED walls, physical props, stunt rigs, costumes, makeup, prosthetics, haze machines, rain machines, and controlled VFX plates
- camera language that a cinematographer could execute
- emotion caught during an action beat, not a flawless static pose
- believable continuity details: same face, same hair, same outfit, consistent lighting/time/place

Avoid:
- AI poster aesthetics
- game cinematic renders
- concept art layouts
- perfect fantasy lighting
- plastic skin and waxy faces
- evenly sharp background detail
- symmetrical model-pose perfection
- decorative words that make the scene feel generated instead of filmed

## Real camera ingredients

Include a small number of concrete camera-capture details:
- camera position: stage floor, handheld at eye level, over-the-shoulder, from the passenger seat, across the street, backstage corridor, audience pit, security-camera angle, etc.
- lens feeling: 24mm wide, 35mm natural environmental lens, 50mm standard, 85mm portrait compression, or telephoto compression
- exposure behavior: blown practical highlights, darker background falloff, mixed color temperatures, visible light spill, natural contrast, slight underexposure in shadows
- physical imperfections: subtle motion softness, lens flare, atmospheric haze, sensor noise-like grain, sweat, dust, rain droplets, fabric wrinkles, imperfect hair strands

Use only what fits the scene. Do not pile every ingredient into every prompt.

## Human realism

For people, include:
- natural skin texture with subtle pores, uneven tone, small highlights, and environmental color spill
- eyes with real catchlights from the scene light sources
- hair with weight, irregular strands, and scene-appropriate movement
- believable hands and body balance
- micro-expression tied to the story beat
- wardrobe that sits on the body with real folds, seams, tension, weight, and reflections

If a face reference exists, photorealism must support the reference face instead of beautifying it into a generic model.

## Non-real elements must be filmed realistically

When the scene contains sci-fi or impossible elements, write them as if filmed on a real production:
- futuristic armor becomes physical costume pieces with seams, scuffs, weight, and limited flexibility
- spaceship interiors become built sets with practical lights, panels, cables, dust, fingerprints, and believable scale
- glowing portals become LED/projection/VFX light sources that cast real spill onto actors and set surfaces
- magical effects become interactive light, smoke, particles, practical wind, and composited VFX that obey lens exposure
- giant concert devices become LED rings, trusses, moving lights, projection mapping, pyrotechnics, haze, and real stage engineering

## Live concert / stadium photography template

Chinese:
```text
真实演唱会现场摄影感，像专业摄影师在体育场内场、摄影坑位或T台前方用真实相机拍下的一帧。舞台灯光来自真实射灯、LED屏幕、T台灯带、观众手机光和荧光棒，混合色温真实地落在人物皮肤、头发和服装上。保留现场拍摄的不完美：轻微镜头眩光、空气薄雾、局部运动柔化、反光舞台地面的真实高光、观众灯光散点和暗部噪声。人物是唯一主焦点，观众和舞台装置作为真实环境退后，不做AI海报式完美渲染。
```

English:
```text
realistic live concert photography, as if captured by a professional photographer from the stadium floor, photographer pit, or runway edge with a real camera. The lighting comes from real spotlights, LED screens, runway light strips, audience phone lights, and light sticks, with mixed color temperatures falling naturally across the singer's skin, hair, and outfit. Preserve live-capture imperfections: subtle lens flare, atmospheric haze, slight motion softness, real highlights on the reflective stage floor, scattered audience lights, and darker shadow noise. The performer remains the only primary focal point while the crowd and stage installation recede as believable environment, not an AI poster render.
```

## Dialogue / short-drama scene template

Chinese:
```text
真实短剧现场拍摄感，像剧组用电影机在真实场景中拍下的一帧。人物处在明确的情绪节拍中，表情克制但有生活感，光源来自场景内可解释的窗光、顶灯、路灯、车灯、手机屏幕或实际灯具。画面允许轻微不完美：自然阴影、皮肤细微瑕疵、衣服褶皱、环境杂物、背景轻微虚化和真实曝光限制。
```

English:
```text
realistic AI short-drama production still, as if filmed by a crew on a real location. The character is caught in a clear emotional beat with a restrained lived-in expression. Light is motivated by explainable sources in the scene: window light, ceiling fixtures, street lamps, car headlights, phone screens, or practical lamps. Allow natural imperfections: real shadows, subtle skin imperfections, clothing wrinkles, lived-in background objects, gentle background falloff, and believable exposure limits.
```

## Realism self-check

Before returning a prompt, ask:
- Could this physically be filmed or photographed?
- Where is the camera?
- What real light sources create the image?
- Do the shadows, reflections, contact points, and scale make sense?
- Does the subject look like a real person instead of an AI model?
- Are non-real elements grounded as props, sets, practical effects, or believable VFX?
